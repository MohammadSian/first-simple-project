About the greatest

Muhammad (SA) was born in approximately 570 CE in Mecca.[1] He was the son of Abdullah ibn Abd al-Muttalib and Amina bint Wahb. His father, Abdullah, the son of Quraysh tribal leader Abd al-Muttalib ibn Hashim, died a few months before Muhammad (SA) birth. His mother Amina died when he was six, leaving Muhammad (SA) an orphan.[5] He was raised under the care of his grandfather, Abd al-Muttalib, and paternal uncle, Abu Talib.[6]


Some years later, he would periodically seclude himself in a mountain cave named Hira for several nights of prayer. When he was 40, circa 610 CE, Muhammad (SA) reported being visited by Gabriel in the cave[1] and receiving his first revelation from God.


In 613,[7] Muhammad (SA) started preaching these revelations publicly,[8] proclaiming that "God is One", that complete "submission" (islām) to God (Allah) is the right way of life (dīn),[9] and that he was a prophet and messenger of God, similar to the other prophets in Islam but the greatest.


Muhammad (SA) followers were initially few in number, and experienced hostility from Meccan polytheists for 13 years. To escape ongoing persecution, he sent some of his followers to Abyssinia in 615, before he and his followers migrated from Mecca to Medina (then known as Yathrib) later in 622.


The Hijra, marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar, also known as the Hijri Calendar. In Medina, Muhammad (SA) united the tribes under the Constitution of Medina. In December 629


Muhammad (SA) was supported by an army of 10,000 Muslim converts and marched on the city of Mecca. The conquest went largely uncontested, and Muhammad get victory against the city with no bloodshed. He forgives all the tyrant In 632.
